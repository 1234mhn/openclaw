## P2 第一段 · 引入

🎵 音频：节目开头 00:00-01:00

**原文：**
Becca: Hello. Welcome to 6 Minute English from BBC Learning English. I'm Becca.
Georgie: And I'm Georgie. Now, Becca, do you read a lot?
Becca: Hmm, I don't read often. I just feel like I don't have the time.
Georgie: I would love to read more, but I don't read very much at the moment. I read mostly before bed because I feel like it helps me relax and go to sleep.

**中文大意：**
主持人Becca和Georgie在聊阅读习惯，两人都承认自己读得不多。Georgie说睡前阅读能帮助放松入睡。

**词汇讲解：**

① **relax** /rɪˈlæks/ v. 放松
- 英英：to rest and become calm
- 例句：Reading before bed helps me relax.

## P3 第二段 · 阅读不是天生的

🎵 音频：约 01:00-02:30

**原文：**
Becca: We might think of reading as natural, like speaking. But Maryanne Wolf, author of the book Reader Come Home, says this isn't true.
Maryanne Wolf: We think of language as natural, and reading is written language so it must be natural. But it isn't natural at all.
Becca: Scientific studies suggest that when we're born, our brains already have the networks that allow our eyes to see and our vocal cords to produce sounds, but not with the pathways we need to read.

**中文大意：**
我们以为阅读和说话一样是天生的。但科学家说：不对。人类出生时大脑有看和说的回路，但没有阅读的回路。阅读能力需要后天专门训练才能建立。

---

## P4 词汇讲解①

**natural** /ˈnætʃərəl/ adj. 自然的、天生的
- 英英：coming from nature; developed without special training
- 例句：Language is natural for humans, but reading is not.
- 反义词：unnatural 不自然的

**dedicated** /ˈdedɪkeɪtɪd/ adj. 专用的、专门的
- 英英：designed and used for one particular purpose
- 例句：Our brain doesn't have a dedicated reading area.
- 拓展：dedicate v. 致力于 / dedication n. 奉献

**co-opt** /kəʊˈɒpt/ v. 征用、借用
- 英英：to involve someone or something, sometimes against their will
- 例句：To read, we co-opt parts of the brain designed for other tasks.
- 语境：阅读时大脑"征用"了原本用于视觉和听觉的区域

## P5 第三段 · 阅读是"全脑运动"

🎵 音频：约 02:30-04:00

**原文：**
Rebecca Gotlieb: From an evolutionary timescale, our brain hasn't had enough time to develop a dedicated reading brain. And so, to build a reading brain network, we co-opt parts of the brain involved in vision and auditory processing, and language, and attention and affect. Reading is really a whole brain process. It involves activation in all four lobes of the cortex. The process of developing a reading brain alters everything, from brain activity to brain structure and brain connectivity. The power of deep reading is really fundamental to our humanity. When we read deeply, we change our brains and we change who we are.

**中文大意：**
神经科学家说：从进化角度看，大脑还没时间专门发展出"阅读脑"。所以阅读需要征用大脑中原本用于视觉、听觉、语言、注意力和情绪的区域。阅读是全脑活动，调动大脑皮层的所有四个脑叶。深度阅读会改变大脑活动、大脑结构、大脑连接——"当我们深度阅读时，我们改变了大脑，也改变了我们自己。"

**词汇讲解：**

**circuit** /ˈsɜːkɪt/ n. 回路、电路
- 英英：a system of connections
- 例句：Reading builds new circuits in the brain.

**inevitably** /ɪnˈevɪtəbli/ adv. 不可避免地
- 英英：in a way that cannot be stopped or avoided
- 例句：Learning a new language inevitably changes your brain.

**intricate** /ˈɪntrɪkət/ adj. 复杂的、精细的
- 英英：having lots of detail
- 例句：Chinese characters are beautifully intricate symbols.

## P6 第四段 · 不同语言塑造不同大脑

🎵 音频：约 04:00-05:30

**原文：**
Georgie: The language we read also shapes our brain. Chinese characters, for example, use symbols instead of letters of the alphabet to represent words and ideas.
Becca: Research suggests that learning to read these symbols activates different areas of the brain to reading an alphabet-based system.
Georgie: Scientists studied a bilingual man who could read and speak Chinese and English. The man suffered a stroke, which affected parts of his brain, including his ability to read Chinese. But amazingly, he was still able to read English.
Maryanne Wolf: It's a beautiful example of how the brain's circuit reflects the requirements of Chinese, which inevitably means more visual memory and visual processing of those beautifully intricate symbols or characters.

**中文大意：**
读不同语言会塑造不同的大脑。汉字用符号表意，阅读汉字会激活更多视觉记忆和视觉处理区域。科学家发现一位中英双语者中风后失去了阅读中文的能力，但还能读英文——因为两种语言在大脑中走的"回路"不同。

## P8 句型仿写

**句型①**
原文：Reading is really a whole brain process.
结构：[动作] is really a whole [名词] process
仿写：
1. Learning a language is really a whole brain process.
2. Playing music is really a whole brain process.

**句型②**
原文：When we read deeply, we change our brains and we change who we are.
结构：When we [动作], we change [对象] and we change [深层影响]
仿写：
1. When we travel, we change our perspective and we change who we are.
2. When we learn a new skill, we change our abilities and we change our confidence.

原文：The language we read also shapes our brain.
结构：[事物] we [动作] also shapes [对象]
